## Section 1: Headline Numbers

**The apr28 kinetics calibration degraded full-stack validation performance. Zero of 14 mixes now pass S0 5/5, down from 5 of 14 in the pre-apr28 baseline. Every mix in the library is classified REGRESSED. No mix improved.**

- **S0 5/5 count**: 0/14 post-apr28 vs. 5/14 pre-apr28. The five mixes that were previously validated (reference group: MIX-01, MIX-02, MIX-03, MIX-11, MIX-12) all regressed, losing between 3 and 4 of their 5 passing gates.
- **Previously failing mixes now in envelope**: Zero. None of the 9 mixes that were failing pre-apr28 crossed into S0 5/5 compliance. Several showed partial movements in individual metrics but none improved their gate count to 5/5.
- **Largest single improvement**: MIX-09 CornerRMS improved by −2.30°F (from 4.57°F to 2.27°F). MIX-07 PeakGrad Δ improved by −3.26°F in magnitude (from +4.97°F to −1.71°F). These partial improvements occurred within mixes that also regressed severely on PeakMax.
- **Regressions**: Every mix regressed. The dominant pattern is a uniform ~3.5°F increase in PeakMax Δ magnitude (engine under-predicts CW peak by 3–4°F more than pre-apr28) and a ~2.5°F increase in CenterRMS across reference mixes (from the documented ~0.74°F floor to ~3.2–3.3°F). PeakGrad Δ worsened for most mixes; CornerRMS partially improved for 10 of 14 mixes.
- **Did the calibration close the kinetics gaps?** No. The apr28 calibration that passed 14/15 mixes adiabatically has universally worsened full-stack accuracy. The likely explanation is error cancellation break: pre-apr28, the engine's over-estimated kinetics offset the BC layer's over-estimated heat loss, yielding near-correct full-stack peaks. The calibration corrected kinetics in isolation without accounting for this interaction, removing the heat that was previously compensating for BC over-correction.

## Section 3: Residual Attribution

**PeakMax failures (14/14 mixes)**: Every mix fails PeakMax S0 post-apr28. The residuals are uniformly negative (engine under-predicts CW peak by 2–7°F), with reference mixes clustered tightly at −3.5 ± 0.3°F. This uniformity is diagnostic: a per-mix or per-group effect would produce varied magnitudes and possibly mixed signs. Instead, the −3.5°F shift is nearly constant across reference mixes regardless of SCM percentage, cement type, or placement temp variant. The dominant candidate is an energy budget under-supply introduced by the apr28 calibration. The calibration corrected Hu for the adiabatic regime; in the full-stack regime the BC layer removes additional heat, and the pre-apr28 engine was apparently compensating for BC over-loss by over-generating kinetic heat. With kinetics corrected, the BC over-loss is unmasked. A secondary candidate for the cluster_a mixes is partial over-correction of the positive PeakMax residuals (MIX-07 flipped from +4.12°F to −4.88°F), but since reference mixes with near-zero pre-apr28 residuals shifted by the same ~3.5°F, over-correction alone does not explain the uniform shift.

**PeakGrad failures (13/14 mixes — only MIX-02 and MIX-06 and MIX-10 pass)**: PeakGrad failures are split between two regimes. For reference and b1 mixes (MIX-01, 03, 04, 08, 11, 12), PeakGrad worsened in the negative direction, consistent with the engine under-predicting peak temperature more severely (lower peak → smaller gradient from center to edge → more under-prediction of peak gradient). For cluster_a mixes that were previously over-predicting (MIX-06, 07, 10), PeakGrad improved or maintained — the sign flipped from positive to negative and in some cases the magnitude decreased. The three PeakGrad passes (MIX-02 at −0.84°F, MIX-06 at −1.90°F, MIX-10 at −1.52°F) are cluster or Type-V mixes where the pre-apr28 positive gradient residual partially offset the new negative energy deficit. The candidate cause for PeakGrad failures in reference/b1 mixes is the same energy budget under-supply driving PeakMax; R_form and soil model effects are secondary candidates but cannot be distinguished from this data alone.

**CenterRMS failures (14/14 mixes)**: CenterRMS post-apr28 ranges from 1.42°F (MIX-14) to 5.48°F (MIX-15), vs. a pre-apr28 range of 0.57°F (MIX-02 reference) to 4.84°F (MIX-09). For reference mixes, CenterRMS jumped from the documented 0.74°F structural floor to ~3.2°F. The CenterRMS metric captures the RMS error of the centerline temperature trace over the evaluation window (48–168 hours). The uniform ~2.5°F increase for reference mixes implies that the engine's centerline temperature trace is now running persistently ~2.5°F below CW across the evaluation window, not just at peak. This is consistent with lower kinetics energy supply reducing the concrete's temperature throughout the entire hydration phase, not just at peak. The top-surface BC physics is a documented contributor to CenterRMS (the 0.74°F v3 floor), but the additional ~2.5°F post-apr28 residual appears to originate in energy supply, not in BC amplitude oscillation. This is an important distinction: fixing the top-surface BC physics as currently documented would not close the 3.2°F CenterRMS gap seen post-apr28.

**CornerRMS outcomes (mixed — 10 mixes improved, 4 mixes worsened or stayed)**: CornerRMS improved for MIX-01, 03, 04, 05, 06, 08, 09, 11, 12, and 14. It worsened for MIX-07, 10 (slightly), 15, and 02 (slightly). The partial CornerRMS improvement is counterintuitive given that all other metrics worsened. A possible explanation: corner cells are most affected by form-side and ambient boundary conditions; if the engine's corner temperature was previously elevated by kinetics over-prediction (pushing corner temps higher relative to boundary conditions), reducing kinetics lowers corner temps closer to boundary-moderated values, which happen to be closer to CW's corner temps. This interpretation would be consistent with the apr28 calibration correcting a kinetics-driven corner over-prediction. The mixes where CornerRMS worsened (MIX-07, 15, 02) are either extreme-SCM (91.3%) or extreme-placement-temp (45°F), where the corner temperature dynamics differ from the reference regime.

**FieldRMS failures (14/14 mixes)**: FieldRMS is a superposition of the above. The uniform increase in FieldRMS across all mixes (from 0.87–4.15°F to 2.07–6.79°F) is driven primarily by the PeakMax and CenterRMS degradation. MIX-14 is the only mix where FieldRMS improved (3.20°F → 2.07°F), coinciding with its partial improvements in CenterRMS and CornerRMS.

## Section 4: Sprint 7 Routing Recommendation

**Primary recommendation: Option 2 — Top-surface BC physics ablation**

Fourteen of 14 mixes now fail CenterRMS, with reference mixes showing CenterRMS at 3.2–3.3°F vs. the documented v3 0.74°F floor. The systematic ~3.5°F under-prediction of PeakMax across reference mixes — mixes that were near-zero before the calibration — cannot be explained by kinetics residuals alone (the calibration was validated adiabatically). The most consistent explanation is that the engine's BC layer is removing too much heat in full-stack mode: the top-surface, form-side, and soil boundary conditions collectively suppress the engine temperature below CW's temperature by ~3.5°F after the kinetics correction removes the compensating over-prediction. A BC physics ablation study — disabling or reducing BC heat-loss terms individually and observing which term accounts for the 3.5°F gap — is the only way to isolate whether this is top-surface convection, form-side R-value, or soil conduction. Without this ablation, the energy budget imbalance between adiabatic-calibrated kinetics and full-stack BC loss remains unresolved, and any further kinetics or geometry tuning will be operating blind.

**Runner-up: Option 3 — Soil model investigation (cold-placement regime)**

MIX-15's post-apr28 residuals (PeakMax −6.34°F, PeakGrad −10.49°F, FieldRMS 6.79°F, CenterRMS 5.48°F) are dramatically worse than all other mixes, suggesting a physics interaction specific to the cold-placement regime that amplifies the baseline energy deficit. The cold-placement regime (45°F initial temp) slows the hydration rise significantly; if the soil boundary condition is modeled as a fixed-temperature sink appropriate for summer conditions but not winter-to-spring soil thermal conditions, the cold-placement run would lose heat through the bottom at an unrealistically high rate, compounding the kinetics-side under-prediction. Resolving MIX-15 may require both the BC ablation (primary) and a separate investigation of the soil thermal model for cold-placement conditions. MIX-15 is de-prioritized as primary because its residuals likely contain the same ~3.5°F energy-budget offset seen in reference mixes, plus a cold-placement-specific additional term; the primary ablation study should be conducted first to isolate the baseline offset.

## Section 5: Open Questions and Risks

**1. Error cancellation break — needs explicit confirmation.** The uniform ~3.5°F downward shift in PeakMax for reference mixes that were near zero pre-apr28 is consistent with the hypothesis that pre-apr28 kinetics over-prediction was compensating for BC over-loss. If this is confirmed, it means the sprint-4 baseline (5/14 passing) was achieved through error cancellation, not because the BC and geometry physics were correct. This would mean the full-stack validation was never truly passing on physical grounds — it was a coincidence. Confirming or refuting this requires a ablation run with apr28 kinetics + BC terms reduced to see if the gap closes. Do not treat the sprint-4 5/14 baseline as a physics-correct reference — it may have been a false positive.

**2. The apr28 adiabatic validation passed 14/15 mixes but full-stack has 0/15 passing — the gap is larger than expected.** The apr28 release notes document MIX-07's pre-apr28 adiabatic residual as +4.12°F (engine over CW). Post-apr28 full-stack, MIX-07's PeakMax Δ is −4.88°F. The expected trajectory was: adiabatic calibration closes the +4.12°F → ~0°F, full-stack adds BC effects on top of corrected kinetics → ~0°F ± some residual. Instead, MIX-07 ended up at −4.88°F. The ~9°F swing (from +4.12°F to −4.88°F) is much larger than the ~0°F adiabatic residual would suggest. This implies the full-stack CW peak for MIX-07 is substantially different from the adiabatic CW peak, which suggests CW's own BC response changes the peak significantly in their full-geometry run. This is worth investigating: if CW's full-stack peak is significantly higher than its adiabatic peak for the same mix (because CW models heat retention differently in full geometry), the engine needs to match the full-geometry CW output, not the adiabatic CW output.

**3. KineticsCorrectionWarning fired for MIX-09.** The loader emitted a warning: "Silica fume 20.8% exceeds the 15% envelope." This means MIX-09's Hu calibration is being applied outside its validated composition range. MIX-09's post-apr28 results should be treated as provisional. The partial metric improvements seen in MIX-09 (FieldRMS, CenterRMS, CornerRMS) may not be reproducible or physically meaningful — they may reflect an accidental partial correction in an unvalidated SF regime.

**4. MIX-14's partial improvement is anomalous.** MIX-14 (75°F warm placement) is the only mix where FieldRMS, CenterRMS, and CornerRMS all improved despite being REGRESSED overall. The warm placement temperature interacts differently with the heat budget: higher initial temperature means the hydration exotherm's contribution to the absolute peak is relatively smaller. It is unclear whether the partial improvement reflects a genuine calibration benefit for high-placement-temp scenarios or an accidental compensating interaction. This should be noted as a data point for the BC ablation study.

**5. No per-mix CSV or JSON detail files were generated.** `run_all.py` does not natively emit per-mix detailed outputs (CSV, JSON). Only the aggregate markdown was written. The per-mix temperature traces and gate-level detail are available in the `--quiet`-suppressed stdout and optionally via `--png-dir`; if per-mix trace analysis becomes relevant during Sprint 7, the run should be re-executed with `--png-dir validation/post_apr28_full_stack/plots/` to produce visual diagnostics.

**6. Hard stop flag.** Per the task brief: "If you find something unexpected, document it in the report and stop — do not attempt to fix it." The finding that 0/14 mixes pass S0 post-calibration, down from 5/14, is the unexpected finding. This report documents it. No source code changes were made. No physics tuning was attempted. The report and comparison table are the deliverables; the decision about what to do next belongs to the engineer.
